Definition

  • Quiddity: what something is (what species, what specific traits, what universal)
  • Haecceity: why it is this particular thing (specimen, how indicated as a particular)
  • Ipseity: how it is itself (subject), apperception, self aware.

A shift from the usage in medieval philosophy to the usage in analytic philosophy.

Medieval usage, Duns Scotus

  • haecceity is species, the essence of a kind
  • quiddity is particularity, the essence of an individual

Analytic metaphysics often separates:

  • objects
  • properties
  • relations

So the questions become:

  • What individuates **objects**? → haecceity
  • What individuates **properties**? → quiddity

Compare fungible, numerical identity
