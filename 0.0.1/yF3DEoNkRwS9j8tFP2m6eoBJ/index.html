Definition

discernment as a synonym for Kantian Intution. C I Lewis Given. John Vervaeke "relevance realization. Pure Intuition is akin to Locke's primary qualities. The beheld is "recognized as". Sentient praxis uses beholding as one of its tools. Qualia has reverted to a version of sense-data in analytic philosophy. To say there is no beholding is to say there is no sentience. There are no vertebrates with complex nervous systems. This is an esoteric philosophical position which has, unfortunately become popular among a certain type of materialist-idealist sophisticates. And so the border between the esoteric and the exoteric is blurred.

Objects and granularity are combinatarily explosive. So one oubliates that which is not discerned.

Corroborative, iterative interpretation across domains to identify salient patterns relevant to norms.

This is a really fertile move. You’re essentially trying to **name a cross-traditional “middle layer” of mind**—something between raw sensation and explicit judgment—and using *discernment* as the umbrella. That can work, but only if you’re clear about what gets unified and what gets flattened.

Let me map this carefully.

1. What “discernment” would be naming (your umbrella)

If you use **discernment** as a unifying term, you’re implicitly defining it as:

> **the (often pre-reflective) capacity by which a situation is structured as meaningful, salient, and action-relevant**

That gives you three components:

  • **selection** (what stands out)
  • **structuring** (how it shows up)
  • **orientation** (what matters for action/judgment)

This is exactly the “middle layer”:

  • not raw input
  • not explicit reasoning
  • but what makes reasoning possible

2. Mapping your specific terms

Let’s see how well they fit under *discernment*.

John Vervaeke — *Relevance Realization*

  • Closest match
  • Basically:
  • dynamic selection of salience
  • context-sensitive importance

Fits very well:

> Discernment ≈ lived, ongoing relevance realization

Slight loss:

  • Vervaeke’s emphasis on recursive, system-level dynamics

Immanuel Kant — *Intuition (Anschauung)*

  • For Kant:
  • intuition = **immediate givenness of objects in space/time**
  • structured by forms of sensibility

artial fit:

  • Discernment includes **selection and significance**
  • Kantian intuition is more:
  • **receptive**
  • less evaluative

👉 So:

> Discernment = intuition **plus** relevance-structuring

---

🧠 Edmund Husserl — *Noema*

  • Noema = the **object as intended/experienced**
  • Already structured, meaningful

✅ Good fit:

  • Discernment can be seen as:
  • what *constitutes* the noema as meaningful

👉 Strong overlap:

> Both live in the “appearance-as-meaningful” space

---

🧠 C. I. Lewis — *The Given*

  • The “given” = immediate experiential content
  • But already shaped by conceptual interpretation

⚠️ Partial fit:

  • Discernment is more **active/selective**
  • The given is more:
  • passive
  • foundational

👉 So:

> Discernment operates *on and within* the given

---

🧠 Michael Graziano — *Attention Schema Theory*

  • The brain models its own attention
  • Produces awareness as a simplified representation

⚠️ Indirect fit:

  • This is a **mechanistic explanation** of something like discernment
  • Not the phenomenon itself

👉 So:

> Discernment = the lived phenomenon

> Graziano = a theory explaining how it might arise

---

🧭 3. What you’re actually unifying

Across these traditions, you’re identifying a shared concern:

> **How does the world show up as meaningful before explicit thought?**

Different traditions slice it differently:

| Tradition | Focus |

| --------- | ---------------------------- |

| Kant | structure of intuition |

| Husserl | intentional content (noema) |

| Lewis | experiential given |

| Vervaeke | dynamic relevance |

| Graziano | neural modeling of attention |

Your move:

> Collapse these into a functional unity = **discernment**

---

⚠️ 4. The risk of flattening

This is the main philosophical danger:

You risk erasing key distinctions:

  • **receptive vs. active**
  • **phenomenological vs. mechanistic**
  • **normative vs. descriptive**

Example:

  • Kant: conditions of possibility
  • Graziano: computational model

These are not just different *answers*—they’re different *levels of explanation*

If you do this well, you get a powerful unifying move:

And:

> I will call this structuring **discernment**.
