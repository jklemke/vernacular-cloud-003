Definition

That is "situated, contingent recursion". A **situated (space-time indexed), local (bounded neighborhood), history-cumulative, iterative reconfiguration of constraints that both closes and opens possibilities**—only knowable in its unfolding. Ontological iteration with a transformative reconfigurations of constraints and opportunities.

**Recursion:** the situated, time-indexed process by which a system’s current state locally reshapes the constraints governing its subsequent states, such that the space of possibilities is partially reconstituted at each iteration.

Two important clarifications to explicitly include in the paper:

  • **Not fixed-rule recursion** (against Alan Turing-style readings)
  • **Not self-reference only** (against Douglas Hofstadter-style popular interpretations)

You are using recursion as **ontological iteration with constraint transformation**.

Our definition is meant to blocks both:

  • **determinism** (no full specification at the start), and
  • **teleology** (no end-state doing explanatory work)

But a time-indexed actualization of possibility and impossibility: the iterative establishment off new constraints and new potentials.

Some modes of recursion

  • sustained (externally sustained) like a whirlpool, and eddy. transfer of energy from outside
  • stabilized (self stabilized) star
  • metastable (threshold of collapse: sandpile, avalanche, market crash, neural consciousness)
  • reproductive (virus)
  • maintained (normative enaction)

Recursive iteration is by nature inductive. It gives us a rule to apply at the next iteration. It does not give a rule applicable to the ENTIRETY. That would be the antinomy of Universal Necessity. the Myth of the Proven.
